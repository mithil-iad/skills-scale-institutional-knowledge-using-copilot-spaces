# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

## UX Designer

### Role Summary
UX Designers are responsible for user research, interaction design, and ensuring that product experiences are intuitive and accessible. They collaborate closely with Product Managers and Developers to translate requirements into usable interfaces.

### Responsibilities
- Conduct user research and usability testing
- Create wireframes, prototypes, and design specifications
- Define and maintain design systems and component libraries
- Collaborate with developers to ensure accurate implementation
- Advocate for accessibility and inclusive design standards

### Goals
- Deliver intuitive, accessible, and delightful user experiences
- Reduce friction and support task completion for end users
- Maintain a consistent look and feel across the product

### Typical Communication
- Design reviews and critique sessions with engineering and product
- Handoff notes and annotated mockups in design tools
- User research readouts shared with the broader team

---

## DevOps Engineer

### Role Summary
DevOps Engineers build and maintain the infrastructure, CI/CD pipelines, and deployment tooling that enable teams to ship reliably and rapidly. They bridge development and operations to improve system reliability and deployment frequency.

### Responsibilities
- Design, implement, and maintain CI/CD pipelines and automation
- Manage cloud infrastructure, environments, and access controls
- Monitor system health, respond to incidents, and conduct post-mortems
- Enforce security and compliance standards in the deployment process
- Support developer productivity through tooling and environment improvements

### Goals
- Enable fast, safe, and repeatable deployments
- Minimize toil and reduce mean time to recovery (MTTR)
- Maintain high availability and observability for production systems

### Typical Communication
- Incident response coordination and post-incident reviews
- Infrastructure change notifications and deployment announcements
- Collaboration with developers on environment and pipeline requirements

---

## Business Analyst

### Role Summary
Business Analysts bridge the gap between business needs and technical solutions. They gather and document requirements, analyze processes, and ensure that delivered solutions align with organizational goals.

### Responsibilities
- Elicit, document, and validate business and functional requirements
- Analyze existing processes and identify opportunities for improvement
- Create user stories, process flows, and acceptance criteria
- Facilitate workshops and stakeholder interviews
- Validate that delivered solutions meet business requirements

### Goals
- Ensure technical solutions align with business objectives
- Reduce ambiguity and rework by clarifying requirements early
- Improve cross-functional understanding of business processes

### Typical Communication
- Requirements documentation and user story grooming with Product and Engineering
- Stakeholder workshops and discovery sessions
- Sign-off on acceptance criteria alongside Product Manager

---

## Support Lead

### Role Summary
The Support Lead ensures that customers receive timely, effective assistance and that product issues are captured and escalated appropriately. They act as the voice of the customer within the delivery team.

### Responsibilities
- Manage and triage customer support tickets and escalations
- Communicate known issues and workarounds to customers and internal teams
- Collaborate with Engineering and QA to reproduce and prioritize reported bugs
- Contribute to release readiness by reviewing release notes and support materials
- Track support trends and share insights with Product and Project Managers

### Goals
- Minimize customer impact from product issues
- Reduce ticket volume through proactive communication and documentation
- Drive continuous improvement by surfacing recurring customer pain points

### Typical Communication
- Escalations to Engineering and PM for critical customer-impacting issues
- Release and incident updates shared with support team and customers
- Regular reporting on support metrics to Product and Project Managers
