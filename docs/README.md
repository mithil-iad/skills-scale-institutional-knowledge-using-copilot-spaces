# OctoAcme Project Management Processes

This repository centralizes OctoAcme's project management processes, providing a single source of truth for the entire team. It enables consistent execution, transparent communication, and continuous improvement across cross-functional projects.

## Overview

OctoAcme’s processes guide project delivery from initiation through release and retrospective. The lifecycle begins with defining the business need, aligning stakeholders, and setting measurable goals. Planning transforms those goals into actionable backlogs with prioritized tasks, acceptance criteria, and a risk register. Execution is managed with daily standups, weekly delivery syncs, and structured project boards, while quality assurance is enforced through regular testing, CI tooling, and manual QA for critical flows. Releases are standardized with checklists and rollback plans, and improvement is supported by retrospectives, with action items tracked in the backlog.

## Key Workflows

- **Project Lifecycle:** Initiate, plan, execute, release, and retrospect, supported by templates and checklists for key artifacts (one-pagers, risk lists, release notes).
- **Work Execution:** Tasks move through Backlog, Ready, In Progress, QA, and Done columns. Small, traceable pull requests link to issues and require CI checks and approval before merging.
- **Escalation:** Blockers are triaged at team level, escalated by PMs as needed, and sponsor-level attention is sought for business-critical issues.
- **Continuous Improvement:** Regular retrospectives capture learnings and drive actionable improvements.

## Roles and Personas

- **Project Manager (PM):** Orchestrates delivery, schedules, risks, and communications.
- **Product Manager (PdM):** Owns vision, outcomes, and prioritization.
- **Developers:** Implement features/fixes, maintain tests, and collaborate on design.
- **QA:** Validate features against standards for acceptance.
- **UX Designer:** Leads user research and interaction design to ensure usable, accessible experiences.
- **DevOps Engineer:** Builds and maintains CI/CD pipelines, infrastructure, and deployment tooling.
- **Business Analyst:** Bridges business needs and technical solutions through requirements documentation and process analysis.
- **Support Lead:** Manages customer escalations, communicates known issues, and surfaces customer feedback to the team.
- **Stakeholders:** Provide input, review, and approvals.

For full role definitions and interaction patterns, see [Roles & Personas](octoacme-roles-and-personas.md).

## Communication Strategies

- Daily standups, weekly syncs, monthly stakeholder updates, and ad-hoc escalations.
- Status, risk, and progress tracking through project boards and dashboards.
- Stakeholder communication is structured and transparent, supporting psychological safety and alignment.

## Quality Assurance Practices

- Unit, integration, and end-to-end tests for new work and critical flows.
- Security scanning as part of CI.
- Manual QA for feature acceptance.
- Release checklists include smoke tests, backup/rollback steps, and stakeholder announcements.

## Continuous Improvement

- Retrospectives after each milestone or incident.
- Action items tracked in backlog/issues, with impact reviewed in regular team syncs.
